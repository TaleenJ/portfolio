## About {#about}

I knew I wanted to do computer science back in sixth grade. I learned to code with Scratch and built a four-player soccer game kids could play on a tablet — and that was it. I was hooked. Programming suddenly felt like a way to make something fun that other people could jump into and enjoy.

I earned my B.S. in Computer Science from CSU Fullerton (May 2026), and now I'm pursuing my M.S. in Computer Science at California State University, Long Beach. What I love most is data work from start to finish: collecting and cleaning, building pipelines, evaluating models, and sharing findings in a way that actually helps someone.

I'm bilingual (English and Spanish). I swam competitively for four years in high school, and I still love being in the water. Swimming taught me that the little things matter — timing, form, focus — and those same details are what make you faster. That mindset sticks with me in data and code. Outside of class I teach swimming, and I'm always looking for data science clubs and hackathons where I can build with other people and keep learning.
